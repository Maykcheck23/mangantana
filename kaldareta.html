Kaldareta Baka History

Kaldereta is a popular Filipino stew with strong Spanish influences, evidenced by its name derived from "caldera" (cauldron) and the use of ingredients like tomato sauce, bell peppers, and sometimes even cheese or liver spread. While traditionally made with goat meat, beef (baka) is a very common and popular substitute, especially in urban areas. The dish reflects the Filipino adaptation of Spanish stews, incorporating local flavors and techniques, often resulting in a rich, savory, and slightly spicy stew, particularly when chilies are added./p>

Kaldareta Baka Recipe

  • 1 kg beef stewing cuts (e.g., short ribs, chuck), cut into 2-inch cubes
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large bell pepper (red or green), cut into strips
  • 1 large potato, peeled and cubed
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and sliced
  • 1 can (8 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup liver spread (or peanut butter for a thicker sauce)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2-3 green chilies (siling haba), optional
  • 1-2 cups beef broth or water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Optional: cheese

Kaldareta Baka Procedure

  1. Brown Beef: Heat cooking oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown the beef cubes on all sides. Remove beef and set aside.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: In the same pot, sauté minced garlic and chopped onions until fragrant and softened.
  3. Return Beef and Simmer: Return the browned beef to the pot. Pour in tomato sauce, soy sauce, beef broth (or water), and green chilies (if using). Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until beef is very tender. Add more liquid if necessary to prevent drying.
  4. Add Vegetables and Flavorings: Once beef is tender, add potatoes and carrots. Simmer until vegetables are cooked through. Stir in liver spread (or peanut butter) and bell pepper strips. Continue to simmer for another 5-10 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken and flavors to meld.
  5. Season and Serve: Season with salt and black pepper to taste. If desired, stir in grated cheese or olives just before serving. Serve hot with steamed rice.
