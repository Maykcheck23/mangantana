Puto Lansungan History

Puto Lansungan is a steamed rice cake that originates from Biñan, Laguna. Unlike other puto varieties, it is cooked using a lansungan, a traditional steamer covered with muslin cloth. The batter, made from rice flour, brown sugar, and baking powder, is poured into the steamer and cooked over rice husk-fired ovens. This method gives the puto a distinct texture—fluffy yet moist. Historically, Puto Lansungan was sold at the Biñan Station of the South Main Line of the Philippine National Railways, where vendors would hop onto train coaches to offer it as a merienda or pasalubong. Over time, it became a well-known delicacy, often topped with grated cheese, salted egg bits, and condensed milk for added flavor. This dish reflects Filipino ingenuity, combining traditional cooking methods with modern adaptations. It remains a beloved snack, showcasing the rich culinary heritage of Laguna.

Puto Lansungan Ingredients

  • Cassava
  • Grated Coconut
  • Brown Sugar
  • Violet Food Color
  • Buko

Puto Lansungan Recipe

  • 1 kilo Grated Cassava (Kamoteng kahoy)
  • 1 Grated Coconut (Murang Niyog)
  • 1/4 kilo Brown sugar or Muscuvado sugar
  • Violet food color
  • 1 buko
  • 1/4 brown Sugar

Puto Lansungan Procedure

  1. Kudkurin ang Kamoteng kahoy hindi Pino dapat yung medyo malaki butas ng kudkuran o yung susunod sa pinakapino na pangkudkod
  2. Mix lang ang Kinudkod na kamoteng kahoy at ang kinayod na niyog saka brown sugar saka ube food color or kung gusto mo condened milk ube flavor kung may budget kapa
  3. Lagyan ng Palaman na bukayo sa hulmahan na meron ka. Pwede ang bao ng niyog o molde o kahit bilugin mo lang at ilagay sa dahon ng saging ayos na
  4. Steam lang sa 45 minuto hanggang maluto sa katamtamang apoy
