Rendang History

Beef Rendang is a rich and aromatic dry curry originating from the Minangkabau people of West Sumatra, Indonesia, and is also very popular in Malaysia and Singapore. While not a traditional Filipino dish, its popularity has grown in the Philippines due to the shared culinary heritage and the love for rich, flavorful stews. Rendang is characterized by its slow cooking process, where beef is braised in coconut milk and a paste of mixed spices until the liquid evaporates and the meat becomes tender, absorbing all the flavors.

Rendang Recipe

  • 1 kg beef stewing cuts (e.g., chuck, brisket), cut into 2-inch cubes
  • 2-3 cups thick coconut milk (first extract)
  • 1 cup thin coconut milk (second extract)
  • 10-12 red chilies (adjust to spice preference)
  • 1 large onion, roughly chopped
  • 6-8 cloves garlic
  • 2-inch ginger, sliced
  • 2-inch galangal, sliced
  • 3-4 stalks lemongrass (white part), sliced
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 4-5 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1-2 tablespoons tamarind paste (or a squeeze of lime juice)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (palm sugar preferred)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil

Rendang Procedure

  1. Make Spice Paste: Blend all spice paste ingredients with a little water or oil until a smooth paste is formed.
  2. Sauté Spice Paste: Heat cooking oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Sauté the spice paste until fragrant and the oil separates, about 8-10 minutes.
  3. Brown Beef: Add beef cubes to the pot and cook, stirring, until lightly browned and coated with the spice paste.
  4. Add Liquids and Simmer: Pour in the thin coconut milk, add kaffir lime leaves and bay leaves. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until beef is nearly tender.
  5. Add Thick Coconut Milk and Finish: Add the thick coconut milk, tamarind paste, and sugar. Stir well. Continue to simmer, uncovered, stirring frequently to prevent sticking, until the liquid reduces and the sauce thickens and oil renders from the coconut milk. This process can take another 1 to 2 hours, depending on the heat. The rendang is done when the sauce is almost dry and coats the beef beautifully.
  6. Season and Serve: Season with salt to taste. Serve hot with steamed rice.
