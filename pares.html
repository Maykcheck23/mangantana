Pares History

Beef Pares is a popular Filipino comfort food, typically consisting of braised beef (often beef briskets or chuck) in a sweet-savory soy sauce-based broth, served with garlic fried rice ("sinangag") and a clear beef broth or soup. The term "pares" literally means "pairs," referring to the pairing of the beef and rice, often with the soup. It's a relatively modern street food and carinderia (eatery) staple, gaining popularity in the latter half of the 20th century as a quick, affordable, and satisfying meal.

Pares Recipe

  • 1 kg beef brisket or chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2-3 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick (optional)
  • 1/2 cup beef broth or water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • For garlic fried rice: cooked rice, minced garlic, cooking oil

Pares Procedure

  1. Prepare Beef: In a bowl, combine beef cubes, soy sauce, and black pepper. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Sauté minced garlic and chopped onions until fragrant and softened.
  3. Brown Beef: Add the marinated beef (reserving the marinade) to the pot and brown on all sides.
  4. Simmer Beef: Pour in the reserved marinade, brown sugar, star anise, cinnamon stick (if using), and beef broth (or water). Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until beef is very tender and the sauce has thickened to a syrupy consistency. Stir occasionally.
  5. Prepare Garlic Rice: While beef is simmering, prepare garlic fried rice by heating oil in a pan, sautéing minced garlic until golden and fragrant, then adding cooked rice and stirring until heated through and well combined. Season with salt.
  6. Prepare Soup: If serving with soup, simmer beef bones or beef cubes in water with salt and pepper until a clear broth is formed.
  7. Serve: Ladle the tender beef pares over a serving of garlic fried rice. Garnish with chopped green onions. Serve with the clear beef soup on the side.
