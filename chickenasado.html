Chicken Asado History

Chicken Asado Kapampangan is a sweet and savory braised chicken dish originating from Pampanga, known as the "Culinary Capital of the Philippines." Unlike the more common pork asado, the Kapampangan version often features chicken and a richer, slightly sweeter sauce with distinct star anise and soy sauce notes. It's a staple in Kapampangan households for special occasions and daily meals, reflecting the region's sophisticated palate.

Chicken Asado Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Soy Sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Brown Sugar
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Star Anise
  • Cinnamon Stick
  • Chicken broth or water
  • Cooking Oil
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Optional: Bay Leaf, Bell pepper

Chicken Asado Recipe

  • 1.5 kg chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar (cane or rice vinegar)
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2-3 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick (optional)
  • 1 cup chicken broth or water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: bay leaf, bell pepper strips

Chicken Asado Procedure

  1. Marinate: In a bowl, combine chicken, soy sauce, vinegar, minced garlic, and black pepper. Marinate for at least 30 minutes, or preferably an hour in the refrigerator.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Sauté sliced onions until softened. Add the marinated chicken (reserving the marinade) and brown on all sides.
  3. Braise: Pour in the reserved marinade, chicken broth (or water), brown sugar, star anise, and cinnamon stick (if using). Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until chicken is tender and cooked through.
  4. Thicken Sauce: Remove the chicken and continue to simmer the sauce, uncovered, until it thickens to your desired consistency. You can also dissolve a teaspoon of cornstarch in a little water and add it to the sauce to thicken faster.
  5. Serve: Return the chicken to the pot to coat with the sauce. Serve hot with steamed rice.
