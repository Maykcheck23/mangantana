Inihaw na Bulig History

The history of Inihaw Na Dalag traces back through generations, showcasing the enduring culinary heritage of Filipino cuisine. Inihaw na Dalag, which means grilled mudfish, has been a beloved dish in Filipino cuisine for centuries. The tradition of grilling the mudfish over charcoal has been passed down through families, symbolizing the cultural significance of communal gatherings and special occasions.asoning. This traditional method highlights Filipino love for smoky, charred flavors, emphasizing simplicity in cooking while maximizing taste. Inihaw na bulig remains a popular dish in rural communities, often enjoyed during gatherings or beach outings as a flavorful yet straightforward meal.

Inihaw na Bulig Procedure

  1. Prepare the fish by removing the guts and gills before grilling.
  2. Wash the fish thoroughly to clean it before cooking.
  3. Stuff the fish with a mixture of chopped tomato, onion, ginger, lemon juice, and ground black pepper.
  4. Rub salt all over the fish, including its cavity, to add flavor before grilling.
  5. To add extra flavor, grill the fish for 10 to 12 minutes on each side, preferably over a charcoal grill for a smoky flavor.
  6. Serve the grilled Inihaw Na Dalag with steamed rice and a dipping sauce made by combining soy sauce and melted butter.
