Giniling History

Beef Giniling (meaning "ground beef" in Tagalog) is a versatile and humble Filipino dish, primarily made with ground beef and often includes diced potatoes, carrots, and sometimes green peas. It's a common and practical everyday meal, reflecting the Filipino penchant for stews and stir-fries that are easy to prepare and feed a family. While its exact historical origin is unrecorded, it likely evolved as an economical and convenient way to utilize ground meat, influenced by the Spanish use of sofrito (sautéed aromatics) and tomato-based sauces.

Giniling Ingredients

  • Ground Beef
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Potato
  • Carrot
  • Tomato Sauce
  • Green Peas
  • Beefbroth or Water
  • Cooking Oil
  • Soy Sauce
  • Salt & Pepper

Giniling Recipe

  • 500g ground beef
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large potato, diced
  • 1 large carrot, diced
  • 1 can (8 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1/2 cup beef broth or water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Fish sauce (patis) or soy sauce to taste
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Giniling Procedure

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a large pan or pot over medium heat. Sauté minced garlic and chopped onions until fragrant and softened.
  2. Brown Ground Beef: Add ground beef to the pan. Cook, breaking up the meat with a spoon, until browned and no longer pink. Drain excess fat if necessary.
  3. Add Vegetables and Liquids: Add diced potatoes and carrots. Stir for a few minutes. Pour in tomato sauce and beef broth (or water). Bring to a simmer.
  4. Simmer and Season: Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the potatoes and carrots are tender. Stir in green peas and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Serve: Season with fish sauce (or soy sauce), salt, and black pepper to taste. Serve hot with steamed rice.
