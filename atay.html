Adobo Atay at Balunbalunan History

Adobo Atay at Balunbalunan (Chicken Liver and Gizzard Adobo) is a resourceful and flavorful rendition of the classic Filipino Adobo. Like all adobo dishes, its origins lie in pre-colonial preservation methods. Using organ meats like liver and gizzard is a testament to Filipino culinary ingenuity, ensuring no part of the animal goes to waste. This specific adobo variant is prized for its rich, earthy flavors and distinct textures, often appearing as a pulutan (bar snack) or a viand for everyday meals.

Adobo Atay at Balunbalunan Recipe

  • 500g chicken liver, cleaned
  • 500g chicken gizzard, cleaned and trimmed
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, crushed
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1-2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1/2 cup water or chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup water or chicken broth

Adobo Atay at Balunbalunan Procedure

  1. Prepare Gizzards: Place chicken gizzards in a pot with water, bring to a boil, and simmer for about 30-45 minutes until tender. Drain and set aside. (This step ensures gizzards are tender as they take longer to cook than liver).
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a pan or pot over medium heat. Sauté crushed garlic and chopped onions until fragrant.
  3. Add Gizzards: Add the pre-cooked chicken gizzards and cook for a few minutes.
  4. Add Liver: Add the chicken liver and cook until they change color, about 2-3 minutes. Avoid overcooking the liver to prevent it from becoming tough
  5. Simmer: Pour in soy sauce, vinegar, water (or broth), bay leaves, and black peppercorns. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the sauce has reduced and thickened
  6. Serve: Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Serve hot with steamed rice.
