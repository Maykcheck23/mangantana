Chicken Isaw History

Isaw refers to grilled chicken or pork intestines, a popular street food in the Philippines. Its origins are humble, rooted in the practice of utilizing every edible part of an animal, particularly among less privileged communities. Over time, "isaw" evolved from a necessity into a beloved snack, often enjoyed with various dipping sauces. Chicken isaw offers a milder flavor compared to its pork counterpart, and both are quintessential Filipino street food experiences.

  • Vinegar

  • Calamansi Juice
  • Brown Sugar
  • Garlic
  • Black Peppercorn
  • Skewers
  • For dipping sauce: vinegar, soy sauce, minced garlic, chopped chilies, sugar

Chicken Isaw Recipe

  • 1 kg chicken intestines, thoroughly cleaned
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons calamansi juice (or lemon/lime juice)
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns, crushed
  • Skewers
  • For dipping sauce: vinegar, soy sauce, minced garlic, chopped chilies, sugar

Chicken Isaw Procedure

  1. Clean Intestines Thoroughly: This is the most crucial step. Rinse intestines multiple times under running water. You can also turn them inside out to clean thoroughly. Boil in water with a little vinegar and salt for 10-15 minutes to remove impurities and any strong odor. Drain and rinse again. Repeat boiling if necessary until clean and tender-crisp.
  2. Marinate: In a bowl, combine vinegar, soy sauce, calamansi juice, brown sugar, minced garlic, and crushed black peppercorns. Add the cleaned chicken intestines and marinate for at least 1-2 hours, or overnight in the refrigerator.
  3. Skewer: Thread the marinated intestines onto bamboo skewers, folding them accordion-style
  4. Grill: Preheat a charcoal grill or an outdoor grill. Grill the chicken isaw over medium-high heat, basting frequently with the leftover marinade, until thoroughly cooked and slightly charred. This usually takes 5-10 minutes per side, depending on the thickness.
  5. Serve: Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.
