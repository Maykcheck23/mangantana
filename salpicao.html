Salpicao History

Beef Salpicao is a flavorful and aromatic Filipino dish that shows a clear Spanish influence, given its name (from "salpicar," meaning to sprinkle or season, or a dish with chopped ingredients). It's characterized by tender beef cubes quickly cooked in a rich, garlicky sauce, often with a hint of Worcestershire sauce and butter. While often served as pulutan (bar snack), it's also a popular main course due to its intense savory flavor and tender meat. It represents the Filipino adaptation of European-style sautéed beef dishes.

Salpicao Ingredients

  • Beef Tenderloin
  • Garlic
  • Olive Oil
  • Butter
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Soy Sauce
  • Black Pepper
  • Salt
  • Optional: Red Peppers, Sliced Mushroom

Salpicao Recipe

  • 500g beef tenderloin or sirloin, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 head garlic, minced (a lot!)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns, coarsely crushed
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: sliced mushrooms, red pepper flakes

Salpicao Procedure

  1. Prepare Beef: Pat beef cubes dry with paper towels. Season with salt and coarsely crushed black pepper.
  2. Heat Oil and Garlic: Heat olive oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat. Add half of the minced garlic and sauté until fragrant and slightly golden (be careful not to burn).
  3. Sear Beef: Add the beef cubes to the hot pan in a single layer. Do not overcrowd. Sear quickly on all sides until browned. Remove the beef from the pan and set aside.
  4. Make Sauce: In the same pan, reduce heat to medium. Add the remaining minced garlic and sauté until fragrant. Stir in Worcestershire sauce and soy sauce, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan. Let it simmer for about a minute.
  5. Finish Dish: Return the seared beef to the pan. Add butter and stir until melted and coats the beef. Toss to combine. If using mushrooms, add them now and cook until tender.
  6. Serve: Serve immediately, garnished with a sprinkle of additional crushed black pepper or red pepper flakes if desired. Best served with steamed rice.
