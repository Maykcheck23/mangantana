Tinola History

Tinola Manok is a classic Filipino ginger-based chicken soup, believed to have indigenous origins even before Spanish colonization. It's a comforting and nutritious dish often served as an appetizer or a main course, particularly during cooler weather or when one needs a pick-me-up. Its simplicity and use of fresh, local ingredients like malunggay (moringa) leaves and green papaya reflect traditional Filipino cooking.

Tinola Recipe

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, julienned
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large green papaya, peeled and sliced into wedges (or chayote)
  • 1-2 cups malunggay (moringa) leaves or dahon ng sili (chili leaves)
  • 6-8 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Fish sauce (patis) to taste
  • Salt to taste

Tinola Procedure

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté garlic until fragrant, then add ginger and onions and cook until softened.
  2. Sauté Chicken: Add chicken pieces to the pot and cook until they change color, about 5-7 minutes
  3. Deglaze and Season: Add fish sauce and cook for a minute, stirring well.
  4. Simmer: Pour in the rice washing or chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20-30 minutes, or until chicken is tender. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface.
  5. Add Vegetables: Add the green papaya (or chayote) and continue to simmer until the papaya is tender-crisp.
  6. Add Greens: Stir in the malunggay or dahon ng sili leaves and cook for another 2-3 minutes, or until the leaves are wilted.
  7. Serve: Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Serve hot.
