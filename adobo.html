Adobo sa Gata History

Chicken Adobo sa Gata is a regional variation of the Philippines' national dish, Adobo, with a creamy twist from coconut milk ("gata"). While adobo's origins are deeply rooted in pre-colonial Filipino cooking as a preservation method using vinegar and salt, the addition of coconut milk is popular in Southern Luzon regions like Bicol and Quezon, where coconut is abundant. This version offers a richer, milder, and often slightly spicier flavor profile compared to the classic adobo.

Adobo sa Gata Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Soy Sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Coconut Milk
  • Green Chilies
  • Bay Leaves
  • Black Peppercorn
  • Cooking Oil
  • Salt

Adobo sa Gata Recipe

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, crushed
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cups coconut milk (first and second extract)
  • 2-3 green chilies (siling haba or labuyo), optional
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt to taste

Adobo sa Gata Procedure

  1. Marinate: In a bowl, combine chicken, soy sauce, vinegar, and half of the crushed garlic. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a pot over medium heat. Sauté the remaining crushed garlic and chopped onions until fragrant.
  3. Brown Chicken: Add the marinated chicken (reserving the marinade) to the pot and brown on all sides.
  4. Braise: Pour in the reserved marinade, bay leaves, black peppercorns, and green chilies (if using). Bring to a boil, then add the coconut milk.
  5. Simmer: Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30-45 minutes, or until chicken is tender and the sauce has thickened and oil has rendered from the coconut milk. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  6. Season: Season with salt to taste. Serve hot with steamed rice.
