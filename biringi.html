Biringi History

Biringi is the Kapampangan version of paella or arroz valenciana, indicating its Spanish culinary influence. Originating from Pampanga, it's a festive and elaborate rice dish often prepared for special occasions, fiestas, and gatherings. Unlike traditional Spanish paella, Biringi utilizes glutinous rice ("malagkit") and often includes coconut milk, giving it a richer, creamier texture and a distinct Filipino flavor. It typically features chicken, chorizo, and various vegetables, showcasing the Kapampangan knack for elevating flavors.

Biringi Recipe

  • 2 cups glutinous rice (malagkit)
  • 1 cup regular rice
  • 1 kg chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper (red or green), sliced
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1/4 cup chorizos de Bilbao or Chinese sausage, sliced
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 3-4 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder (for color)
  • 1 tablespoon annatto oil (optional, for color)
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Hard-boiled eggs for garnish

Biringi Procedure

  1. Prepare Rice: Wash glutinous and regular rice together. Set aside.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a large, wide pan or paellera over medium heat. Sauté garlic until fragrant, then add onions and cook until softened.
  3. Brown Chicken: Add chicken pieces and cook until lightly browned. Add chorizo slices and cook for a few minutes.
  4. Add Rice and Liquids: Add the washed rice mixture and stir for 2-3 minutes until coated. Stir in turmeric powder and annatto oil (if using). Pour in coconut milk and chicken broth. Bring to a boil.
  5. Simmer and Cook: Reduce heat to low, cover tightly, and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the liquid is absorbed and the rice is cooked. Do not stir excessively to avoid making the rice mushy.
  6. Add Vegetables: Gently stir in bell pepper strips and green peas during the last 5-10 minutes of cooking.
