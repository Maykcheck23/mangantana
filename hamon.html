Hamun Kapampangan History

raditionally, Kapampangans got.their hamun from the.local Chinese store. It was either Hoc Siu, King Sue.or Marca Pato. The best ham was hamon de funda (whole leg ham bone-in and neatly tied in net stocking). The burgeoning sugar industry of the rich sugar planters and landlords preferred imported Virginia ham. Now local ham is produced and is supplied by several popular homegrown meat processing industries. The Kapampangan is likely to expect ham as a Christmas gift from his boss or trading suki. Employees proudly display their hams on the way home from their last day of work or after their Christmas party. As one popular ham brand puts it, the ham is the "star of the noche buena"

Hamun Kapampangan Ingredients

  • Pork Ham
  • Rock Salt
  • Brown Sugar
  • Prague Salt
  • Pineapple Juice

Hamun Kapampangan Recipe

  • 6 pounds Pork ham (pigue), cut into halves
  • 1/2 cup rock salt
  • 3 1/4 cup rock salt
  • 1 tablespoon Prague salt #1
  • 1 can (1.3 liters) Pineapple juice

Hamun Kapampangan Procedure

  1. Cut the pork in half, rinse, and drain well. Place in a large bowl.
  2. In a small bowl, combine rock salt, ¼ cup of brown sugar, and Prague salt. Stir until evenly distributed.
  3. Rub curing mixture on the pork, massaging well to coat. Transfer pork to an airtight container.
  4. Cure pork for about 5 to 6 days, turning on sides every day. Rinse and drain well.
  5. Place on paper towels to remove excess liquid. Place the pork in a meat net and tie the net tightly to form the pork into shape..
  6. Place the pork in a large pot. Add pineapple juice and 2 ½ cups of brown sugar. Stir until sugar is dissolved and bring to a boil.
  7. Lower heat, cover, and cook for about 1 to 1 ½ hours or until meat is tender. Remove the ham from the pot and using scissors, cut and remove the net while still hot.
  8. Allow to cool and then rub with the remaining ½ cup brown sugar.
  9. Arrange in a single layer on a baking pan and broil until caramelized.
  10. Meanwhile, boil the pineapple and brown sugar mixture until thickened.
  11. Slice the ham into desired thickness and serve with the pineapple glaze.

    Notes

  • The pork will expel liquid during curing as the salt mixture will draw out moisture from the meat.
  • While the ham is still hot, remove the net to prevent it from sticking too much to the meat.
  • The nutritional info includes the pineapple glaze.
