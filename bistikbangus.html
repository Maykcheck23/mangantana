Bistik Bangus History

Bistik bangus is a Filipino take on bistek, originally a beef dish influenced by Spanish stewing techniques. Traditionally, bistek uses thinly sliced beef marinated in soy sauce, calamansi, and onions, creating a balance of savory and citrusy flavors. Filipinos later adapted this recipe using bangus (milkfish), taking advantage of its accessibility and naturally rich taste. Bistek itself has roots in Spanish cuisine, but Filipinos transformed it by incorporating local citrus fruits like calamansi instead of lemons or vinegar. The bangus version follows a similar marinade, making the fish flavorful yet tender. Today, bistik bangus is a great example of Filipino culinary adaptation, showing how local ingredients and ingenuity create fresh versions of classic foreign-inspired dishes.

Bistik Bangus Recipe

  • 0.5 cup Vegetable Oil
  • 1 pc Potato
  • 2 pcs Red Onion
  • 0.5 kg Bangus
  • 1 sachet MAGGI® Magic Sarap® 8g
  • 0.25 cup All Purpose Flour
  • 4 cloves Garlic
  • 1.25 cup Water
  • 3 tbsp MAGGI® Oyster Sauce
  • 2 tbsp Calamansi Juice
  • 1 tbsp Brown Sugar
  • 0.25 tsp Freshly ground pepper
  • 2 tbsp Spring Onion
  • 1 tbsp Toasted Garlic

Bistik Bangus Procedure

  1. Fry potato in preheated oil until golden brown. Remove and set aside
  2. Sauté onion in the same pan. Strain and set aside.
  3. Season bangus with MAGGI® Magic Sarap® and coat well with flour. Fry in the same oil until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  4. Remove oil from the pan leaving 2tbsp. Sauté garlic until light golden brown. Pour water and simmer. Add MAGGI® Oyster Sauce, calamansi juice, sugar and pepper. Add back fried bangus and potato and simmer for 2mins
  5. Transfer on a serving plate, top with sautéed onion, spring onion and toasted garlic. Serve immediately.
