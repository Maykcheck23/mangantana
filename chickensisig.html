Chicken Sisig History

Sisig is a Kapampangan dish, traditionally made from parts of a pig's head (ears, snout, jowls) and liver, seasoned with calamansi and chili. The term "sisig" originally referred to a sour snack. The modern sizzling sisig was popularized by Lucia Cunanan ("Aling Lucing") in Angeles City, Pampanga, in the mid-1970s. Chicken Sisig is a popular alternative, offering a lighter and more accessible version for those who prefer not to eat pork or for healthier options, while still capturing the dish's iconic savory and spicy flavors.

Chicken Sisig Ingredients

  • Chicken Breast
  • Onion
  • Red Chilies
  • Mayonnaise
  • Calamansi Juice
  • Soy Sauce
  • Liquid Seasoning
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Cooking Oil

Chicker Sisig Recipe

  • 1 kg chicken breast or thigh fillets
  • 1 large onion, minced
  • 1-2 red chilies (siling labuyo), minced (adjust to spice preference)
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup calamansi juice (or lemon/lime juice)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1-2 tablespoons liquid seasoning (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil

Chicken Sisig Procedure

  1. Cook Chicken: Boil chicken fillets in salted water until cooked through. Drain and let cool slightly. Shred or finely chop the chicken. Alternatively, you can grill or pan-fry the chicken for added flavor.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: In a pan, heat cooking oil over medium heat. Sauté half of the minced onions until softened.
  3. Combine Ingredients: Add the shredded chicken to the pan and cook for a few minutes until lightly browned. Transfer the chicken to a large bowl.
  4. Season: Add the remaining minced onions, minced red chilies, mayonnaise, calamansi juice, soy sauce, and liquid seasoning (if using) to the chicken. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly distributed. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  5. Serve Sizzling: Heat a sizzling plate over high heat until smoking. Carefully transfer the chicken sisig mixture to the sizzling plate. Serve immediately, garnished with chopped spring onions or a fried egg.
