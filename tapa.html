Beef Tapa History

Beef Tapa is a quintessential Filipino breakfast food, part of the popular "silog" meals (tapa-sinangag-itlog). The word "tapa" refers to dried or cured meat, a traditional method of food preservation in the Philippines, predating refrigeration. While historically sun-dried, modern beef tapa is typically marinated in a savory-sweet-garlicky sauce and pan-fried, sometimes still with a slight chewiness reminiscent of its preserved origins. It's a beloved staple for its satisfying flavor and quick preparation.

Beef Tapa Ingredients

  • Beef Sirloin
  • Soy Sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Brown Sugar
  • Garlic
  • Black Pepper
  • Cooking Oil

Beef Tapa Recipe

  • 500g beef sirloin or tenderloin, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 head garlic, minced or crushed
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil (for frying)

Beef Tapa Procedure

  1. Marinate Beef: In a bowl, combine beef slices, soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar, minced garlic, and black pepper. Mix well to ensure all beef slices are coated. Marinate for at least 30 minutes, or preferably 2-4 hours, or overnight in the refrigerator for deeper flavor.
  2. Pan-Fry Tapa: Heat cooking oil in a non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the marinated beef slices in a single layer. Do not overcrowd the pan; cook in batches if necessary
  3. Cook Until Browned: Cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until lightly browned and cooked to your desired doneness. Some prefer it slightly caramelized and crispy edges.
  4. Serve: Remove from pan and serve hot. Traditionally served with garlic fried rice (sinangag), fried egg (itlog), and a side of sliced tomatoes or vinegar with chilies.
