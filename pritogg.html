Prito Galunggung History

Galunggong is one of the most affordable fish in the Philippines, often referred to as the “poor man's fish” due to its accessibility and nutritional value. Historically, galunggong has been a staple protein source for Filipino households, frequently fried to create a crispy, flavorful meal. Frying enhances its natural savory taste, making it a reliable everyday dish that pairs well with rice and vinegar. Given its affordability and abundance in local waters, galunggong holds a strong cultural significance, symbolizing practicality and sustenance for many generations.

Prito Galunggung Ingredients

  • Galunggong
  • Salt
  • Ground Black Pepper
  • Oil

Prito Galunggung Recipe

  • 20 pcs galunggong / mackerel scad
  • Salt
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • Oil

Prito Galunggung Procedure

  1. Prepare your galunggong by seasoning it generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat up your wok then add oil, add enough amount for frying your fish. Once oil is hot gently drop the fish one by one and cook until golden brown on both sides.
  3. Serve.
