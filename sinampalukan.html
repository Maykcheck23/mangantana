Sinampalukang Manok History

Sinampalukan Manok is a sour and savory chicken soup that takes its distinct tanginess from young tamarind leaves ("sampalok" in Tagalog). It's a rustic Filipino dish, similar to sinigang but specifically utilizing the leaves rather than the fruit's pulp for souring. This dish highlights the Filipino preference for sour flavors, believed to be a refreshing and appetite-stimulating element, especially in warm climates. It's a comforting soup often prepared in rural areas where fresh tamarind leaves are readily available.

Sinampalukang Manok Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Ginger
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Green chilies
  • Chicken broth
  • Cooking Oil
  • Fish Sauce
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Optional: Kangkong or green beans

Sinampalukang Manok Recipe

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 2-3 cups young tamarind leaves, pounded or roughly chopped
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, julienned
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 green chilies (siling haba), optional
  • 8-10 cups rice washing or chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Fish sauce (patis) to taste
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional vegetables: green beans, kangkong (water spinach)

Sinampalukang Manok Procedure

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté garlic until fragrant, then add ginger and onions and cook until softened
  2. Sauté Chicken: Add chicken pieces to the pot and cook until they change color, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Deglaze and Season: Add fish sauce and cook for a minute, stirring well.
  4. Simmer: Pour in the rice washing or chicken broth and green chilies (if using). Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20-30 minutes, or until chicken is tender. Skim off any scum.
  5. Add Tamarind Leaves: Add the young tamarind leaves and simmer for another 10-15 minutes, or until the leaves have released their sourness and the soup has achieved its desired tang. If using other vegetables, add them now and cook until tender.
  6. Serve: Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Serve hot.
