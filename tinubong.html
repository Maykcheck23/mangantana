Tinubong a Manok History

While "Tinubong" traditionally refers to sticky rice cakes cooked in bamboo, applying the "tinubong" concept to chicken would suggest a cooking method where chicken and its ingredients are sealed and cooked inside a bamboo tube over fire or embers. This method is ancient and traditional in many parts of Southeast Asia, utilizing bamboo as a natural pressure cooker and infusing the food with its subtle aroma. This could be a very rustic, indigenous way of preparing chicken, particularly in areas where bamboo is abundant.

Tinubong a Manok Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Lemon Grass
  • Ginger
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Green Chilies
  • Coconut Milk
  • Fish Sauce
  • Bamboo Tubes
  • Banana Leaves

Tinubong a Manok Recipe

  • 500g chicken pieces (preferably small cuts or boneless for easier fitting in bamboo)
  • 2-3 stalks lemongrass, pounded
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, julienned
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 green chilies (siling haba), optional
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk (optional, for richness)
  • Fish sauce or salt to taste
  • Fresh bamboo tubes (with nodes at one end)
  • Banana leaves for sealing

Tinubong a Manok Procedure

  1. Prepare Bamboo: Cut fresh bamboo stalks into segments, ensuring one end has a node to form a natural base. Clean the inside of the bamboo tubes thoroughly
  2. Prepare Chicken: Combine chicken pieces with lemongrass, ginger, onion, garlic, green chilies (if using), coconut milk (if using), and fish sauce or salt. Mix well.
  3. Stuff Bamboo: Carefully stuff the marinated chicken mixture into the bamboo tubes. Do not overfill.
  4. Seal: Seal the open end of the bamboo tube with a banana leaf or corn husk, securing it tightly.
  5. Cook: Place the bamboo tubes directly over hot embers or on a grill, turning occasionally, for 45 minutes to 1.5 hours, depending on the heat and size of the bamboo.
