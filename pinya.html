Pininyahang Manok History

Pininyahang Manok, or Pineapple Chicken, is a Filipino chicken dish that combines savory flavors with the sweet and tangy notes of pineapple. While its exact origins are not precisely documented, it reflects the Spanish influence on Filipino cuisine (using cooking methods like stewing) and the abundance of pineapples in the Philippines. The dish is a testament to Filipino culinary creativity, balancing the richness of chicken with the refreshing zest of pineapple, often with the addition of milk or coconut milk for a creamy texture.

Pininyahang Manok Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Pinneapple Chunk
  • Pinneapple Juice
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Evaporated Milk or Coconut Milk
  • Fish Sauce
  • Cooking Oil
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Optional: Bell Peper, Carrots, Potatoes

Pininyahang Manok Recipe

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 can (20 oz) pineapple chunks, drained (reserve juice)
  • 1/2 cup pineapple juice (from the canned pineapple or fresh)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup evaporated milk or coconut milk
  • 1-2 tablespoons fish sauce (patis)
  • 1-2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: bell pepper strips, carrots, potatoes

Pininyahang Manok Procedure

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a pot over medium heat. Sauté garlic until fragrant, then add onions and cook until softened.
  2. Brown Chicken: Add chicken pieces to the pot and cook until they change color and are lightly browned on all sides.
  3. Season and Simmer: Add fish sauce and cook for a minute, stirring well. Pour in the reserved pineapple juice. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20-30 minutes, or until chicken is tender.
  4. Add Pineapple and Milk: Add the pineapple chunks and evaporated milk or coconut milk. Simmer for another 5-10 minutes, or until the sauce has slightly thickened and flavors have melded. If using optional vegetables, add them earlier with the chicken to cook until tender.
  5. Serve: Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Serve hot with steamed rice.
