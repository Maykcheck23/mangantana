Sarciado Tilapya History

Sarciadong tilapia is a dish that combines frying and stewing, reflecting both local and Spanish influences. The word "sarciado" is derived from the Spanish term "salsa," meaning sauce, referring to how fish is simmered in tomatoes, onions, and garlic after being fried. This technique became a popular method of repurposing leftover fried fish, making it flavorful and moist instead of dry. Spanish culinary traditions introduced stewed tomato-based dishes, which Filipinos adapted using common ingredients like tilapia. Over time, eggs were added to enhance the sauce, creating a richer texture. Today, sarciadong tilapia remains a comforting and resourceful dish, showing how Filipino households maximize ingredients while maintaining taste and quality.

Sarciado Tilapya Recipe

  • 2 lbs Tilapia cleaned
  • 2 tomatoes diced
  • 2 onions diced
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 3 green onions chopped (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons garlic minced
  • 2 eggs beaten
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • fish sauce to taste

Sarciado Tilapya Procedure

  1. Heat oil in a frying pan then fry the fish. Ensure that salt has been rubbed over the fish before frying.
  2. Remove the fish from the frying pan and place in a a plate with paper towels. Set aside.
  3. Using a clean pan, heat about 2 teaspoons of cooking oil then saut√© the garlic, onions, and tomatoes
  4. Add the fish sauce and ground black pepper then stir.
  5. Put-in the chopped green onions and water then bring to a boil.
  6. Add the fried fish and simmer for 3 to 5 minutes.
  7. Pour the beaten eggs on the pan. Ensure that it is distributed evenly.
  8. Once the eggs curdles or coagulates, you may start stirring the mixture.
  9. Simmer for another 2 minutes than transfer to a serving plate.
  10. Serve hot. Share and enjoy!
