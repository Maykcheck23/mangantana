Bopis History

Bopis is a traditional Filipino dish that comes from the Kapampangan region. It’s made from pork lungs and heart, which are finely chopped and sautéed with onions, garlic, and various spices. The dish has a unique, slightly tangy flavor because of the addition of vinegar, and it often has a bit of heat from chili peppers.

Bopis Recipe

    For Boiling

  • 3 liters Water
  • 350 grams Pork Liver
  • 450 grams Pork Lungs
  • 250 grams Pork Kidney
  • 150 grams Pork Heart
  • 4 stalks Lemon Grass
  • 1 tablespoon Black Pepper
  • 5 cloves Garlic
  • 1 medium Ginger

    • For sauteing

  • 2 tablespoons Cooking Oil
  • 1 medium Ginger
  • 8 cloves Garlic
  • 1 large Onion
  • 1 tablespoon Black Pepper
  • 2 pieces Bay Leaves
  • 2 tablesppons Annato Oil
  • 2 tablespoons Fish Sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Soy Sauce
  • 1/2 cup Water
  • 4 tablespoons Vinegar
  • 1 medium Carrot
  • 1 medium Radish
  • 1 piece Red Bell Pepper
  • 1 piece Green Bell Pepper
  • 2 pieces Green Chilis
  • 2 pieces Red Chilis
  • Salt to taste

Bopis Procedure

    Pre Boiling the Organs

  1. Clean the pork liver, lungs, kidney, and heart thoroughly.
  2. In a large pot, add the pork organs, lemongrass, whole peppercorns, crushed garlic, and sliced ginger. Fill the pot with 3 liters of water or enough to cover the organs.
  3. Bring to a boil and let it simmer for 45 minutes or until the organs are tender.
  4. Once cooked, remove the organs from the pot and let them cool. Dice them into small pieces.

    5. Sauteing

  5. Heat Cooking oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  6. Add minced ginger, minced garlic, and minced onion. Sauté until the onion becomes translucent and the garlic is fragrant.
  7. Add the diced pork organs to the pan. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Mix the crushed black pepper and bay leaves. Add the annatto oil and let it simmer.
  9. Pour in the fish sauce,soy sauce and water. Stir well to combine.
  10. Add the vinegar and let it simmer without stirring for about 2-3 minutes to cook off the acidity.
  11. Add the diced carrot, radish, and bell pepper. Cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  12. Add the sliced green and red chilies. Stir and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  13. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  14. Continue to cook for another 5-10 minutes until all the vegetables are tender and the flavors are well combined.
